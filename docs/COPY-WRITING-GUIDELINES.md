# Copy Writing Guidelines — Avoiding AI-Generated Patterns

*Last updated: May 2026*

---

## The Core Problem

AI-generated copy is detectable by both tools (GPTZero, Originality.ai) and experienced readers. It harms credibility, reduces trust, and is increasingly penalised by Google's Helpful Content system.

**No tool will reliably fix AI copy.** Undetectable.ai, Humanize.ai, etc. produce garbled output that reads worse than the original. The only fix is targeted rewriting with a specific voice.

---

## Telltale AI Patterns to Avoid

### 1. The "Not X. Built for Y." Construction
**AI version:** "Not adapted from a Western product. Built from scratch for how India actually works."  
**Human version:** "Google Forms won't add UPI. Typeform won't add WhatsApp. We did. Because we're Indian."

The contrast sentence (`Not X. Built for Y.`) is extremely common in GPT-4 output. Avoid it.

### 2. Fake Statistics
**AI version:** "3× more leads", "92% completion rate", "70% faster screening"  
**Human version:** Remove entirely, or replace with a factual feature tag ("Conditional logic", "Resume upload")

Made-up numbers with no source destroy credibility with sophisticated visitors. They're worse than no stats at all.

### 3. Fabricated Testimonials
Generic Indian names (Priya Sharma, Rahul Verma) + perfect reviews hitting every feature bullet = immediate trust killer.

**Rule:** No fake testimonials. Ever. Replace with:
- Honest use-case chip clouds ("What people are building")
- Real quotes as soon as they exist, even just 1-2
- A "be our first reviewer" CTA

### 4. Perfect Parallel Structures
**AI version:** Three cards, each with: Badge → Title → Exactly-2-sentence-desc → Detail. Identical rhythm.  
**Human version:** Vary sentence count, vary length, let some items be punchier than others.

AI writes in perfectly parallel patterns. Humans don't.

### 5. Corporate Buzzword Stack
Words/phrases to delete on sight:
- "game-changer"
- "seamlessly"
- "streamline"
- "WhatsApp-native" / "India-first" / "AI-powered" (as adjective stacks)
- "leverage"
- "robust"
- "best-in-class"
- "cutting-edge"

Replace with what the feature *actually does*: "Add a payment field. The money goes directly to your Razorpay account."

### 6. Every CTA Saying "Free" Twice
**AI version:** "Start for free today — it's completely free, no credit card needed, free forever."  
**Human version:** Say it once. Mean it.

### 7. Identical Section Endings
If every page ends with "Free. No credit card. Built for India." — vary it. Pick 2-3 variations and rotate.

### 8. "Plain English" Instruction Descriptions
**AI version:** "Tell the AI what kind of form you need in plain English. No technical knowledge required."  
**Human version:** "Type what you want. 'Feedback form for my restaurant with star ratings.' That's it."

Show the actual thing. Don't describe the thing abstractly.

## What Human Copy Looks Like

### Opinionated and specific
- Names the competitor's actual price
- Has a point of view ("Their support team has never heard of Razorpay.")
- References something specific about the product

### Varied rhythm
- Short sentence. Then a longer one that adds context and colour.
- Not every sentence is the same length.
- Occasional fragment. Intentional.

### Direct address
- "You've probably already tried one of these" not "Indian businesses often struggle with..."
- "The money hits your Razorpay account" not "payments are processed efficiently"

### Founder voice (when appropriate)
- "Because we're Indian. We use the same apps, pay the same way..."
- First-person perspective on *why* decisions were made

---

## Checklist Before Publishing Any Copy

- [ ] Does any sentence use "Not X. Built for Y." → rewrite
- [ ] Are there any made-up statistics? → remove or source them
- [ ] Are there any testimonials? → are they real? if not, replace with something honest
- [ ] Read out loud — does any sentence feel like a product brochure? → rewrite
- [ ] Count "free" in the CTA section — is it said more than twice? → trim
- [ ] Is every list item the same length/structure? → vary at least 2 of them
- [ ] Does the copy show the thing, or describe the thing abstractly? → show it

## Good Reference Voices

Products with consistently human-sounding copy:
- **Linear** (linear.app) — confident, opinionated, no buzzwords
- **Loom** (loom.com) — direct, specific, short sentences
- **Basecamp** (basecamp.com) — opinionated, slightly contrarian, founder voice
- **Notion** (notion.so) — minimal, clean, shows don't tell

---

## When You Get Real Testimonials

The moment you have even 1-2 real quotes from real users:
1. Add them back to the homepage immediately
2. Use the person's actual title and company if possible
3. Avoid editing for grammar — keep natural speech patterns
4. A slightly awkward real quote beats a perfect fake one every time
